Preparing for uni

Isaac Physics is a relatively new website that has many intersting problems sorted by topic and level. The 'extraordinary problems' is particularly worth a look.

3Blue1Brown makes youtube videos which use brilliant graphics to make complex maths topics acessible.

Cambridge Chemistry Challenge is sadly no longer running its monthly online challenge but you can still acess the archive of old challenges, essentially pictoral chemistry riddles, through their website which are extremely fun (though not necessarily very useful).

A-level students

Chemguide is a brilliant resource for chemistry students and probably the best starting place if there is a topic you are struggling with.

Allery Chemistry is a youtube channel with detailed videos revising A-level chemistry topics.

GCSE students

BBC bitesize is a good resource for most GCSE courses for students aiming for GCSE grades up to 7 but generally does not have enough detail for students aiming for grades 8 or 9.

KS2 and KS3 students

The institute of physics has a fun section of at home experiments.

Resources for all ages

CrashCourse make youtube videos on topics ranging from Astronomy to US History.

Khan Academy has been around for a while and can be a useful resource, but beware, the amount of time investment needed to reap any benefits from Khan Academy is substantially higher than most other resources listed on this page.

Code Academy has lots of free courses avaliable if you want to learn to program. In fact, a html course with them was the inspiration behind this website.

UKMT runs maths challenges each year which are suitable for pupils from year 7 and upwards. Past papers are avaliable from their website or in book form and are a great way to entertain and challenge students who are capable mathematitians.
