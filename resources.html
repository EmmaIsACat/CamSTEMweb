Preparing for uni

Isaac Physics is a relatively new website that has many intersting problems sorted by topic and level. The 'extraordinary problems' is particularly worth a look. 3Blue1Brown makes youtube videos which use brilliant graphics to make complex maths topics acessible. Cambridge Chemistry Challenge is sadly no longer running its monthly online challenge but you can still acess the archive of old challenges, essentially pictoral chemistry riddles, through their website which are extremely fun (though not necessarily very useful).

A-level students

Chemguide is a brilliant resource for chemistry students and probably the best starting place if there is a topic you are struggling with. Allery Chemistry is a youtube channel with detailed videos revising A-level chemistry topics

GCSE students

KS2 and KS3 students

The institute of physics has a fun section of at home experiments.

Resources for any age

CrashCourse make youtube videos on topics ranging from Astronomy to US History. Khan Academy has been around for a while and can be a useful resource, but beware, the amount of time investment needed to reap any benefits from Khan Academy is substantially higher than alot of other resources listed on this page. Code Academy has lots of free courses avaliable if you want to learn to program. In fact a html course with them was the inspiration behind this website.
